This painting was ultimately and literally a turning point for the series and is subtitled “Purgatory.”
Initially the color scheme was developed to symbolize the especially mixed Lime green
to the far left and in the center, as overtaking the Death and darkness of the Jungle
to the far right and center. However, as work began intensely to complete the
Jungle sections, or stained glass window sections, an entirely different sense
of the piece and the series developed. A time tunnel of a sort, from childhood to
adult came through the series, and a need arose naturally, to gain a new perspective
on the whole body of work. The vision for the culmination of the series in the
last two paintings developed, as Hell, and Heaven on Earth.

This piece is subtitled Purgatory: Grief. During the painting of this work Jackson
allowed herself to Wikipedia the number of people who died after Saigon fell
and she added that to those who had died during the Viet-Nam War
and those who had died in all the wars around the world
and in World Wars I and II

and she became overcome, anguished by all that loss of life in wars.
She decided that if you fought a war it had better be worth fighting for.
It had better be worth it.

She found in Wikipedia somewhere that the Allies in WWII had agreed
DURING the war upon the Four Freedoms that they were fighting for:

Freedom of Speech * Freedom of Worship * Freedom from Want * Freedom from Fear

And Jackson thought of those in the local and global context now
and how naturally they seem to fit something we could agree upon.

Who can say?
But doesn't that sound like Democracy?

So the series began to be about a lot of things beyond war.

From grief and loss, to clarity and philosophy.
